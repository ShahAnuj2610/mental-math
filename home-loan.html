Home Loan Decision

A clear, India‑focused guide to decide before you take a home loan.

This is a practical checklist with EMI comfort rules, rent comparisons, rate stress tests, and worst‑case safety planning so you can decide with confidence.

EMI comfort

Keep EMI ≤ 30–35% of take‑home.

Rent reality

If EMI is 2× rent, buy only if you’ll stay long‑term.

Job‑loss buffer

Hold 9–12 months of (EMI + essentials), preferably liquid.

Step 1: Safety checks

must‑pass rules

EMI to income

  • Target EMI ≤ 30–35% of take‑home.
  • All EMIs together ≤ 40–45%.
  • Stretching above this raises stress risk.

Down payment

  • Aim for 20%+ down payment.
  • Higher down = lower EMI + interest.
  • Keep funds for registration & setup.

Emergency buffer

  • 6–12 months expenses after down payment.
  • Single income → 12–18 months.

Step 2: Rent vs buy reality

opportunity cost

Rent multiplier

  • EMI ~ rent → buying is easier to justify.
  • EMI 2× rent → buy only if staying 7–10+ years.

Why rent matters

  • EMI − rent = extra monthly cash lock‑in.
  • That extra could be invested or saved.

Break‑even time

  • Transaction costs are high early on.
  • Buying makes sense after 7–10 years.

EMI intuition (India)

quick math

30‑year shortcut

  • Monthly EMI ≈ loan ÷ 200 (around 8–10%).
  • ₹50L → ~₹25k/month.
  • ₹80L → ~₹40k/month.

20‑year shortcut

  • Monthly EMI ≈ loan ÷ 170.
  • Shorter tenure = higher EMI, lower interest.

Rate stress test

  • Check EMI at +1% and +2% interest.
  • If EMI crosses 40%, risk rises fast.

Worst‑case buffer

job loss safety

Corpus formula

  • Corpus = months × (EMI + essential expenses).
  • Baseline safe: 9–12 months.
  • Very safe: 12–18 months.

Keep it liquid

  • Hold buffer in savings/FDs.
  • Avoid locking it in long‑term instruments.

Update yearly

  • Recalculate when EMI or expenses change.
  • Increase buffer if job risk rises.

One‑minute decision script

1) Check EMI ≤ 35%. 2) Stress test +1–2% rate. 3) Compare EMI vs rent. 4) Ensure 9–12 months buffer. 5) Decide only if you’ll stay long‑term.
