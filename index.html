Core shortcuts

fast mental math

Percent math

  • 10% = move decimal left.
  • 5% = half of 10%.
  • 1% = decimal left twice.
  • 15% = 10% + 5%.

APR to monthly

  • Monthly rate ≈ APR ÷ 12.
  • 18% APR → 1.5%/month.
  • 24% APR → 2%/month.
  • 6% APR → 0.5%/month.

Rule of 72

  • Years to double ≈ 72 ÷ return %.
  • 8% → ~9 years.
  • 10% → ~7 years.
  • 12% → ~6 years.

Inflation intuition

  • 3% inflation halves value in ~24 years.
  • 6% halves in ~12 years.
  • Buying power erodes faster than it feels.

Savings & FI

financial independence

4% rule

  • FI number ≈ annual expenses × 25.
  • ₹6L/yr expenses → ₹1.5Cr target.
  • ₹12L/yr expenses → ₹3Cr target.

Saving rate logic

  • Saving 50% = 1 year of expenses saved per year.
  • Saving 25% = 3 years to save 1 year of expenses.
  • More savings = fewer work years needed.

Emergency fund

  • 3–6 months expenses = baseline safety.
  • Unstable income? Target 9–12 months.

Loans & EMI

borrow smart

30‑year EMI shortcut

  • Monthly payment ≈ loan ÷ 200.
  • ₹20,00,000 → ~₹10,000/month.
  • ₹75,00,000 → ~₹37,500/month.

20‑year EMI shortcut

  • Monthly payment ≈ loan ÷ 170.
  • Shorter term = higher EMI, lower interest.

Interest reality check

  • If EMI is barely above monthly interest, principal shrinks slowly.
  • 1% rate drop saves ~1% of principal per year.

Everyday decisions

buy or pass

Cost per use

  • Price ÷ expected uses = value reality.
  • ₹6,000 used 60 times → ₹100/use.

Credit card drag

  • 24% APR ≈ 2% interest per month.
  • ₹10,000 balance → ~₹200/month interest.

Salary multiple

  • If price > 3 months net pay, slow down.
  • Compare financing vs saving 3–6 months.

One‑minute decision script

1) Convert APR to monthly rate. 2) Estimate EMI with shortcuts. 3) Compare to take‑home pay. 4) Translate into “years of expenses saved.” 5) Decide if the tradeoff feels right.
